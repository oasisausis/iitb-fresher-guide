How joining usually works

  • Watch institute webmail and council announcements for orientations, workshops and recruitments.
  • Attend beginner-friendly events and talk to managers, conveners or existing members.
  • For many clubs, regular participation matters more than prior experience.
  • Technical teams may hold separate, year-round recruitment or project selections.

Good starting points for a physics student

  • Krittika — Astronomy Club
  • Maths and Physics Club
  • Electronics and Robotics Club
  • Web and Coding Club
  • Tinkerers' Lab and technical teams

Practical advice: try two or three communities during the first month, then retain the ones whose people and activities genuinely fit you. Avoid joining everything at once.
