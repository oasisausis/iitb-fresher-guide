⚠️ The 'FR' and 'FF' Grades

FR: Awarded for inadequate academic exposure, extremely poor performance, or malpractice[cite: 2]. You must re-register and repeat the course if it is a core subject[cite: 2].

FF: Usually awarded if you miss a seminar/presentation[cite: 2]. You may be permitted a re-exam, but the maximum grade you can get after passing is a DD[cite: 2].

👻 The 'DX' Grade

Awarded if you fail to meet the attendance requirements[cite: 2]. You are barred from taking the end-semester exam[cite: 2]. It doesn't impact your SPI numerically, but it sits on your transcript as a backlog[cite: 2].

🚑 Incomplete (II) & Withdraw (W)

II (Incomplete): Given if you miss the end-sem on medical grounds. You get a 50-mark re-exam later[cite: 2].

W (Withdraw): If a course load is too heavy, you can drop it within the stipulated time (usually 2 weeks after mid-sems). It shows as 'W' and has no impact on your SPI[cite: 2].

📈 Grade Improvement

If your CPI falls below 6.0 and you are put on Academic Probation, you can re-register for courses to improve your grade[cite: 2]. The new grade completely replaces the old one[cite: 2]. Catch: You can only do this for a maximum of TWO courses in your entire degree, and only once per course[cite: 2].

🎧 Audit (AU)

If you just want to learn without the SPI stress, you can Audit a course[cite: 2]. You must maintain 80% attendance to get the 'AU' grade on your transcript[cite: 2]. If you fail the criteria, it's treated as a dropped course[cite: 2].
