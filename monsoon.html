Surviving Monsoons in Mumbai

☔ Essential Packing Reminder

It rains cats and dogs in Mumbai during this time! When coming to campus, you must pack sturdy umbrellas and high-quality raincoats.

Pro-tip: Ensure all your electronics, laptops, and important documents are packed in waterproof bags or layers before you travel.

🍄 Beware the Dampness

Mumbai's extreme humidity means things stay perpetually wet. Leather bags and damp clothes can quickly get ruined by fungi if left unattended in your cupboard. Additionally, food spoils much faster during this season—keep snacks sealed tight and don't hoard perishables.

🧺 Hostel Laundry is Mandatory

Air-drying clothes during the monsoon is nearly impossible and leads to a damp smell. Every hostel has an in-house laundry service. It is highly recommended to give them your clothes so they can be properly dried in their heavy-duty dryers.

🛏️ Leave the Bedding at Home

Do not waste luggage space bringing mattresses or pillows. These are readily sold by vendors just outside the hostels, especially on the main road outside H15 and H16.

🪣 Daily Essentials (H12/13/14)

Need buckets, mugs, toiletries, or quick snacks? The shop cluster around the H12, H13, and H14 area has everything you need to set up your room.
